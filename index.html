The problem

Every bag on the market is built for one device. Yours won't stay that device forever.

A bag bought for one gimbal doesn't fit the next one. Foam cutouts go obsolete the day you upgrade — and generic bags leave everything rattling loose.

01 Brand-specific foam

Cut to one model's exact dimensions. Switch gimbals or camera bodies and the bag is dead weight.

02 Fixed compartments

Only things smaller than the slot fit — and even then, nothing is actually held in place.

03 Oversized generic bags

Room for anything, grip for nothing. Gear shifts and collides in transit.

04 One bag per device

Three gimbal brands means three separate bags — and three separate costs.

The mechanism

Three systems, working together, so the bag adapts and you don't have to.

Inspired by the classic pin-impression board — reapplied, for the first time, as a functional carrying system.

Bilateral pin walls

Two opposing panels of independently sliding pins press in from left and right, capturing your gear's exact profile the moment it's lowered in.

ABS/Nylon rods · silicone base sheet · soft tip caps

Weight-decoupled base

A semi-flexible fiber plate carries the full weight of your gear on its own — so the pins only ever have to grip, never bear gravity.

Fiberglass composite · EVA-lined top surface

Single-pull reset

One exterior pull returns every pin to flat neutral — no unzipping, no reaching inside, no manual reset.

4-string rig · exterior D-ring · ~2–3 sec reset
